LogR Privacy Policy

Last updated April 19, 2026

LogR is a personal CRM app that helps you remember the people who matter most. We built LogR with a simple belief: your relationship data belongs to you and only you.

What Data LogR Stores

LogR stores the information you enter about your contacts and interactions. This includes names, phone numbers, email addresses, birthdays, notes, follow-up reminders, gift ideas, tags, and interaction history. All of this data lives on your device in a local database. None of it is sent to any server.

Where Your Data Lives

Everything stays on your phone. LogR does not have a server, a cloud service, or any backend infrastructure. Your data never leaves your device unless you choose to create an encrypted backup and share it yourself.

Encrypted Backups

LogR lets you create encrypted backups of your data. These backups are protected with AES-256-GCM encryption and a PIN you choose. The encryption key is derived using PBKDF2 with up to 600,000 iterations. Backup files are stored on your device and are only shared if you manually export them. LogR never uploads backups automatically.

Notifications

LogR uses local notifications to remind you about follow-ups and important dates. These notifications are scheduled entirely on your device. No push notification server is involved. Notification content may include contact names and reminder details that you entered.

Biometric and Passcode Protection

LogR offers optional biometric authentication (fingerprint or face recognition) and a 4-digit passcode to protect the app from unauthorized access. Biometric data is handled entirely by your device operating system. LogR never accesses or stores biometric data directly.

Crash Reporting

LogR uses Sentry, a third-party crash reporting service, to help us find and fix bugs that affect app stability. When the app crashes or encounters an error, Sentry automatically collects anonymous technical information including the device model, operating system version, app version, and the error stack trace. This data does not include any of your personal contact information, notes, interactions, or any other content you enter into the app. Crash reports are sent securely to Sentry's servers and are used solely to improve app reliability. You can learn more about Sentry's privacy practices at sentry.io/privacy.

Third-Party Services

LogR uses one third-party service. Sentry receives anonymous crash and error reports to help us improve app stability. No personal data from your contacts, interactions, or notes is ever included in these reports. LogR does not use any advertising, analytics, or other data collection services. There are no SDKs from Facebook, Google Analytics, Firebase, Mixpanel, or any similar service embedded in the app.

Data Collection Summary

  • LogR collects anonymous crash reports to improve stability (via Sentry)
  • LogR does not collect or share any of your personal content
  • LogR does not track your activity
  • LogR does not serve advertisements
  • LogR does not have user accounts or login

Your Control Over Your Data

You are in full control of your data at all times. You can edit or delete any contact, interaction, or follow-up from within the app. You can delete all sample data loaded during onboarding. You can also use the "Delete All My Data" option in Settings to permanently erase everything and reset the app to a fresh state.

Children's Privacy

LogR is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since LogR does not collect any data or transmit anything to a server, there is no mechanism through which children's data could be gathered.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will post the new version at this URL and update the "Last updated" date above. Since LogR does not collect email addresses or have user accounts, we cannot notify you directly of changes. We encourage you to check this page occasionally.
